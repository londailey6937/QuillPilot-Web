EXT. APARTMENT - ESTABLISHING - MORNING

The courtyard apartments are pleasant. Pathways connect each apartment through flowered gardens and sculptured bushes.

INT. APARTMENT

A shrine of mythical creatures - elves, gnomes, and leprechauns - sit with a picture of a man and woman that have the whimsical look of fairies. An urn stands out.

European furniture of a pre-Victorian era adorns the residence. A Tiffany lamp shines on a table.

A young girl in her early twenties stands near the door when we hear a KNOCK. VIDIA wears a long skirt with lace and ribbons - a Shakespearean aesthetic. Flowers adorn her Pixie style hair.

She opens the door to a SHERIFF holding a paper. We see a K-9 patch on his sleeve.

SHERIFF

Elder or Vidia Noobe?

VIDIA

Yes.

We sense his compassion as he hands her an eviction notice.

SHERIFF

I’m sorry I have to give you bad news, but this a Writ of Possession. You have five days to vacate this property.

She manages a weak smile; closes the door and stares at the notice.

ELDER walks into the room. She hands him the notice.

Elder Noobe, mid-twenties, reminds us of Mr. Bean, as he has a slightly outcast aspect to him.

His messy bohemian bob needs a trim, and his flat-toed shoes and clothes are first-rate Goodwill.

A lanyard with a note pad and pen hangs from his neck.

We hear the DRONE of a fly. Elder walks about the room and snatches it with one hand. He walks; opens the door and releases the creature to the morning air.

VIDIA

We have no money left from the will and no place to go. I can’t do this alone anymore. You need to get a job Elder.

EXT. UNEMPLOYMENT OFFICE - ESTABLISHING - MORNING

People are smoking outside. Cars fill the parking lot.

INT. UNEMPLOYMENT OFFICE

Vidia and Elder sit across from a CLERK in a cubicle. Vidia laughs as she plays Pokémon on her cell phone.

The clerk, her face ravaged by neglect and booze, moves paper in the desk trays. She reminds us of a headmistress in a school for girls - business-like and rule bound.

Her cube showcases an array of mythical figures, such as elves and pixies. Four-leaf clovers cover the back of her monitor.

CLERK

I could have sworn I brought your folder in here with me.

She leaves the cube.

Elder picks up a pixie figurine and examines it.

He quickly puts it down; shakes and rubs his hair as if washing it.

He bends down and flakes fall into a coffee cup on the desk. The cup emits a fleeting sparkle.

VIDIA

Leave the pixie alone Elder. We don’t want to cause any trouble today. It’s important that you pay attention.

Elder sits; takes out a sanitizer bottle; wipes his hands and brushes back his hair.

He takes a rubber ball from his pocket and squeezes it.

The clerk comes back with a folder; sits down and takes a drink from the cup.

CLERK

Let’s see. Where were we?

A co-worker walks in and drops some paperwork in the tray.

The clerk’s demeanor has changed - her speech slurred; her appearance gassed-up, as if she just closed down Murphy’s bar.

CLERK (cont'd)

The reward for a job well done is more work.

She laughs.

I have a few jokes about unemployed people. Do you want to hear them?

(beat)

It doesn't matter - none of them work. So, tell me again how I can help you?

VIDIA

My brother needs a real job.

The clerk’s head dips forward to the desk and snaps back as to have awoken up from a nap. She puts on her glasses and looks at his application.

CLERK

This is your brother?

She leans across the desk - breasts touching. Elder straightens up.

VIDIA

He can hear but talks to you with the paper.

She takes off her glasses; cleans them and squints at Elder.

CLERK

I see. What is your name?

Elder writes; shows the note.

The clerk puts on her glasses; ignores the note and looks at the application.

A co-worker stares over the partition into her cube.

CLERK (cont'd)

Well, Newbie. The only job you listed was as a street sweeper. Did they give you a dustpan?

She laughs hysterically.

VIDIA

It’s Noo-Bei. Elder Noobe.

CLERK

(serious)

Were you fired from this job?

Elder writes.

She leans across the desk, and again Elder straightens up.

CLERK (cont'd)

(sheepishly)

I got fired once. Don’t want to brag, but I heard that they changed the employee manual after I left.

(beat)

Do you have a driver’s license?

Elder writes. People walk by and look into her cube.

She leans across the desk, and Elder straightens up.

CLERK (cont'd)

I have to be totally honest with you. There’s no jobs for street sweepers. Can you drive a truck?

Elder looks at her collection. She follows his gaze.

CLERK (cont'd)

Do you believe in fairies and leprechauns?

Elder’s face lights up as he nods. The clerk looks at the monitor as she raises her finger high. It comes down on a key.

CLERK (cont'd)

Well, my lucky day. I found a delivery job for you.

She hands him the printout and takes another drink.

VIDIA

Thank you very much.

Elder and Vidia get up and quickly walk out of the cube.

The clerk, face down on her desk with the cup in her hand, rears up as a supervisor walks in.

Elder and Vidia walk fast towards the exit.

SUPERVISOR

What’s in the cup Delores?

(a pause)

A drink?

CLERK (O.C.)

(words slurring)

No thanks. I already have one.

WAITRESS

You stayed because you’re too lazy to do anything else.
